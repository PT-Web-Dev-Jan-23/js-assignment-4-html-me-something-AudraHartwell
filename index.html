    • Camp Counselor in Minnesota Summer 2014

    After my freshman year of college, I decided that I wanted a non-traditional summer camp job. I wanted to go somewhere new and do something fun. I used a site called to find different opportunities. I ended up getting a job at Camp Lake Hubert for girls in Northern Minnesota. I knew very little about the camp before I packed a bag and headed north to spend 3 months of my life. Luckily it ended up being one of the best times of my life. I even headed back up for summer number two the next summer.

    Being an Au Pair in Australia Summer 2016

    After my second summer at camp, I decided I needed even MORE adventure for my summers. I decided I wanted to visit my Autralia friends from that summer. I found a website that matches families with potential au pairs. For reference, an au pair is basically a live in nanny from a different county. You get paid a small stipend plus room and board to nanny the children, and you get to experience a new country. I found a family, and once again packed my bags and boarded a flight to Brisbane, Australia where I would meet complete strangers and live with them for the next 3 months. I loved my time in Australia. This was my first international flight experience, and I learned a lot of travel tips along the way! Overall it was a great experience, and I returned to college a more worldly individual. 10/10 would recommend.

    Roadtrip across the US May 2017

    After I graduated college, I had exactly 2 weeks until I had to be back in Kirksville, MO to start my masters program. My friend and I had decided we wanted to take a cross-country road trip. We made a list of the places we wanted to go. Mine consisted of Zion National Park, Bryce Canyon National Park, Las Vegas, and the Grand Canyon. Alternatively, her's consisted of In and out. We packed our bags and headed west, much like Lewis and Clark had centuries before us. We had no hotel reservations and no route. From the front passaenger seat, I used my Mom's paper atlas to map our daily drives, and hotels.com to book our hotels each night. Besides a snow storm in Colorado that altered the route, the road trip went surprisingly smooth and we returned seasoned travelers!

    Ragnar Relay in Mount Rainier

    First let me introduce you to what is a "Ragnar Relay." As the name implies, a Ragnar relay is a relay race that lasts roughly 24-30 hours and covers 100-200ish miles. They have two types: road and trail. Basically the trail races consists of 3 loops, that the entirity of your 8 man team runs continuously for roughly 26 hours. This particular race happened to be located in the beautiful Mount Rainier. However, in the summer of 2018 I had no idea what a Ragnar relay was. I had simply received a text message "do you want to run a mountain race with me in August in Washinton?" To which I responded "sure." I really didn't think much of the race until about a week before I was set to leave. I found out I needed a headlamp (because you run the trails at night) and went ahead and picked up some trail shoes. Then I flew out to Seattle to meet my friend and 7 strangers. As a nice warm-up, my friend and I hiked 11 miles in the mountains the day before the race. Well come race day, the only thing I brough to eat was a family size box of cheez its. Luckily there was a race provided dinner. My friend neglected to set up a tent, so we ended up sleeping in his car about a mile from the race site. But...overall the 3 day excursion was incredibly fun and so so so beautiful! I was definitely a lot more prepared for my second Ragnar too!

    #Vanlife in Utah March 2022

    The pandemic had finally come to an end, and my friend and I decided we wanted to explore Utah. But, we did not want to travel the conventional path! We decided that we could just rent a van and travel the entirity of the state. With little research conducted, we packed our bags and flew the Phoenix. We picked up our van and headed out for adventure! The first night we encountered our first trial which was the perplexing issue of timezones inside and outside of Arizona. After pulling over and sleeping on the side of the road somewhere in Utah, we awoke to two different times on our phone and no idea what "time" it actually was. As the week went on, we became #vanlife experts. We slept in walmart parking lots, random highways, a campsite, and a lovely hotel in Salt Lake City. We visited 3 national parks, horsehoe bend, the salt flats, and a whole lot of Red Rocks. We learned to use a coleman grill and go days without showering. One week later, we returned to Phoenix tired, dirty, and with a hefty overcharge for driving way too many miles...totally worth it.

    In summary

    2. You don't have to have a plan for everything.
    3. Most importantly just go do cool shit!
